Background

Visionary Builders started as what we call a simple contractor (a chuck and a truck) taking any job they could get, and building houses was a big part of that. Over time they narrowed their services, moved away from home builds, and wanted a brand that reflects who they are today, not who they were.

Problem

Pull off a brand refresh (not a rebrand!) that a) shed the home builder perception, b) felt true to how far they'd come and where they're headed, and c) never made locals feel like they'd gotten too big for their britches.

Process

Interviewed 10 customers and the entire company, surveyed nearly 100 customers, and completed market research to identify what drives new and repeat business (and what drives home builder perceptions). Validated the findings, built the new branding and strategy from them, launched the brand, updated every touchpoint, created the launch graphics, wrote the blog story and email, and ran a giveaway for merch.

Insight

A good process is the #1 driver of new and repeat business, and it's what separates them from "chuck and a truck" contractors. A vision without a process is just a daydream. They're also known as friendly and down to earth, and they can never lose that as they scale. And to sell $50K custom barns, the look had to level up to match the craftsmanship.

Strategy

Add greens to reflect the down-to-earth nature of the company. Create a badge for the proven process and put it on every customer-facing touchpoint. Update the visual identity, the descriptor, and the tagline to reflect vision AND process, while keeping the soul of the brand intact.

What We Learned

Process and people scored highest. So we built on both.

The Story

Getting the story right, in their voice.

Everything hung on the story: saying who they've become, in their own blue-collar voice, without losing a single local. The blog post is the hub it all came from. I wrote it.

Why it mattered The refresh only works if the story lands. The post tells it straight: same crew, same values, a look that finally matches the craftsmanship. Every channel below pointed back to it.
