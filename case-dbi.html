Background

DBI has spent 40+ years in business. The office furniture industry has changed enormously in that time, and DBI has changed with it. Their brand strategy and visual identity, though, stayed frozen in place.

Problem

DBI's outdated identity sits side by side with their global furniture partner, Haworth, a multibillion dollar company operating in 150+ countries. They needed a visual refresh, and a real answer to their positioning after years of treating it as an afterthought.

Process

Interviewed DBI's senior leaders and team members, reviewed customer data, identified key differentiators, presented to DBI and Haworth leadership, embedded the strategy through DBI's marketing, and developed project profiles to showcase it.

Insight

DBI's old tagline was a window into how they'd approached their brand. "We do office" only said what they do. Not how they do it, or why. And the how and the why are the reasons customers do business with them.

Strategy

Communicate the how and the why through their marketing, and update the visual identity to match the new messaging and Haworth's branding. Storytell through social media and project profiles that showcase their work.
